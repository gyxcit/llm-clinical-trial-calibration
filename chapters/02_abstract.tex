% CONTEXT: Clinical trial matching is a bottleneck in medical research, and
% recent LLM-based systems (TrialGPT) aim to automate the assessment of
% patient eligibility against inclusion/exclusion criteria.

% PROBLEM: Beyond raw accuracy, the probabilistic reliability (calibration)
% of these systems has not been systematically audited, particularly across
% base vs.\ RLHF-aligned models, and across generalist vs.\ medically
% specialized architectures. Moreover, aggregate metrics may hide degenerate
% behaviors on the cases that matter most clinically.

% METHOD: We evaluated 12 configurations (6 open-source LLMs $\times$ 2 prompt
% variants) on 1,015 patient-criterion pairs from TrialGPT-Criterion-Annotations,
% computing Expected Calibration Error (ECE), Brier score, C-index, macro-F1,
% and a composite utility score combining calibration, discrimination, and
% prediction diversity. Four pre-registered hypotheses were tested with
% permutation tests and Bonferroni correction. Stability was verified with
% 5-fold cross-validation and comparison to naïve baselines.

% RESULTS: All 12 configurations exhibit ECE well above the well-calibrated
% threshold (0.05, Van Calster 2019), with values ranging from 0.12 to 0.63.
% RLHF significantly degrades calibration in both Mistral-7B and Llama-3.1-8B
% families ($p < 0.003$ after Bonferroni). Two novel findings emerge from
% deeper analysis: (i) five of the twelve configurations exhibit
% \emph{mode collapse}, predicting ``not enough information'' in more than 97\%
% of cases; (ii) on the 23\% of cases requiring a truly discriminative
% decision, both medical LLMs (Meditron, BioMistral) achieve 0\% accuracy
% despite their apparent superiority on global metrics. Isotonic post-hoc
% recalibration brings 11 of 12 configurations below the calibration threshold
% without retraining.

% CONCLUSION: Aggregate calibration and accuracy metrics can mask complete
% failure of open-source medical LLMs on clinically decisive cases. A
% responsible evaluation protocol must combine calibration, prediction
% diversity, and stratified analysis by case difficulty.

\vspace{0.5cm}
\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} large language models, calibration, clinical trial
matching, RLHF, medical AI, mode collapse, expected calibration error,
conformal prediction
