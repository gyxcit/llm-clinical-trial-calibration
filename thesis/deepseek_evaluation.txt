The re-thresholding diagnostic has been validated on two retrospective corpora.

\paragraph{Theoretical threshold for collapse.}
Let s_pos be the log-probability of the positive label and s_comp be the
log-probability of the strongest competing label. The argmax predicts the
positive label iff s_pos - s_comp > 0.

For a given pair, let μ = E[s_comp - s_pos] be the mean prior gap (the bias)
and σ = sqrt(Var(s_pos)) be the signal amplitude. If we approximate the
distribution of s_pos - s_comp as normal with mean -μ and variance σ^2, then:

P(positive) = Φ(μ/σ)

where Φ is the standard normal CDF. Collapse occurs when this probability
falls below the frequency of the positive class in the dataset (≈0.073 for
inclusion criteria). Solving Φ(μ/σ) < 0.073 gives μ/σ > 1.45, i.e.,
r = σ/μ < 0.69.

The empirical threshold observed in our data (r < 0.6) is consistent with
this approximate derivation. The discrepancy reflects the non-normality of
the log-probability distribution.

\paragraph{Causal direction.} The correlation between r and collapse is
consistent with two causal pathways:

(A) A large prior causes collapse by saturating the decision boundary.
(B) A collapsed model has a frozen argmax, which may artificially inflate
    or deflate the measured prior (a measurement artefact).

Pathway (A) is supported by the re-thresholding intervention: if collapse
were a measurement artefact, replacing the argmax would not recover the
signal. The fact that re-thresholding recovers +0.62 to +0.79 F1 indicates
that the signal is present and the prior is the obstacle — consistent with (A).

A more direct causal test would be to intervene on the prior directly.
This could be achieved by fine-tuning a model with a modified RLHF
objective that explicitly penalizes response prior magnitude.

\section{Clinical Summary: A One-Page Guide}

\paragraph{What we did.}
We evaluated 12 configurations of 6 open-source LLMs on 1,015 patient-criterion
pairs. We measured how well the models' confidence matches their accuracy.

\paragraph{Key findings.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item All models are miscalibrated. This can be fixed by isotonic
          recalibration.
    \item Medical LLMs (Meditron, BioMistral) fail on the hardest 23%
          of cases, despite high global accuracy.
    \item Mode collapse is common (5 of 12 configurations). This is not
          a sign of incompetence; it is a decoding artefact. The signal
          is present in the probabilities but the decision rule masks it.
    \item Re-thresholding recovers the signal (+0.62 to +0.79 macro-F1)
          without retraining.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Recommendations for deployment.}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Before deploying any LLM, run a calibration audit on a held-out
          set of 200-500 cases.
    \item Apply isotonic recalibration if ECE > 0.05.
    \item Check for mode collapse. If prediction diversity < 0.1, apply
          the re-thresholding diagnostic.
    \item Do not rely on global accuracy. Report stratified accuracy.
    \item Maintain human oversight.
\end{enumerate}
